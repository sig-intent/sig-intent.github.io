Why a generalist

In the age of AI, judgment is the bottleneck.

AI can generate any answer in seconds. That makes the answer cheap — and the question expensive. The bottleneck has moved upstream: knowing which problem to solve, which tradeoffs are real, and where a decision will compound. That's a judgment problem, not a tooling problem. Generalists who move fluently between architecture, product, strategy and operations are the ones best placed to make those calls — because the right answer rarely lives inside a single domain.

  • Fluent across architecture, product, strategy and org design.
  • Two decades of pattern recognition across enterprise, consulting and startups.
  • Strong opinions on what to do next; weak attachment to any specific stack or vendor.

About

A polymathic technologist with real depth.

I'm Antonio Elena. I work at the intersection of technology, strategy and systems thinking. Over the past two decades I've worked across enterprise, consulting and startups as an architect, engineering leader and CTO — designing systems, shaping strategy and helping organizations modernize at scale.
